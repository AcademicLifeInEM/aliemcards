ABG versus VBG

ABG

  • PROS: Tells you exactly what the arterial environment looks like (pH, PaCO2, HCO3)
  • CONS: Painful, increased risk of bleeding, hematoma, infection, nerve injury, digitalischemia, injury to staff, delays in care, often requires serial exams

VBG


 Correlation with ABG
pH
  • Good correlation within 0.03-0.04 units [1-4]
pCO2
  • Good correlation [4], although may be non-correlative in severe shock [3]
  • 100% sensitive in detecting arterial hypercarbia in COPD exacerbations using cutoff of PaCO2 45 mmHg [1]
  • Mean difference pCO2 -4 (-12.7 - 4.7 [95%CI]) [4]
HCO3
  • Good correlation
  • Mean difference 0.52 (-1.81 - 2.85 [95%CI]) - 0.9 (-3.35 - 5.09 [95% CI]) [3-4]
Lactate
  • Good correlation
  • Mean difference 0.08 (-0.27 - 0.42 [95%CI]) [3]
Base
excess
  • Good correlation
  • Mean difference 0.19 (-1.86 - 2.24 [95%CI]) [3]

pH assessment in DKA:

  • VBG correlated with ABG well
  • Mean difference -0.015 ± 0.006 units [95% CI]
  • ABG pH changed treatment or disposition in 2.5% cases compared to VBG pH

CONS:

  • May still require ABG if pCO2 is ≥ 45 mm Hg or severe shock

ALGORITHM FOR ORDERING VBG vs. ABG
